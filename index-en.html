What is Sheng Ji

Planting Seeds of Good Fortune for Your Life

Sheng Ji is a fortune adjustment method passed down for thousands of years, aiming to create resonance between personal energy fields and the environment through specific layouts, thereby influencing important areas such as wealth, career, health, and relationships.

Originally, this art was a secret of royal families and nobility, later spreading to the common people, evolving into various forms like life positions, ancestral shrines, and longevity graves, becoming a widely accepted fortune management method in Chinese society. Today, it still receives attention in mainland China, Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan, and Southeast Asia, viewed by many as a method to adjust fortune and enhance life opportunities.

Traditional and Modern Applications of Sheng Ji

Core Principles

The key to Sheng Ji is establishing positive connections between personal information and the external environment through specific layout methods.

  • Using personal biological markers — such as hair, nails, or personal clothing, which carry personal life information
  • Inscribing personal information — such as name, birth date and time, to strengthen the connection between the individual and the layout
  • Specific layout methods — not simply burying items for effectiveness, but requiring professional operation to stabilize the overall layout for continuous effect

Just as a seed needs suitable soil to germinate, the effectiveness of Sheng Ji similarly depends on the correct layout method, not merely burying items for effect.

Evolution from Traditional to Modern

In ancient times, establishing Sheng Ji often required Sheng Ji masters to personally seek dragon veins and identify auspicious spots, followed by specific rituals to ensure the stability of the overall layout.

However, with changes in modern society, traditional Sheng Ji methods are no longer practical for many people. Therefore, modern Sheng Ji, while retaining core essences, has developed forms more suited to contemporary needs, such as tower position Sheng Ji.

Historical and Contemporary Applications

Historically, many notable figures have used Sheng Ji to adjust their fortune. In modern society, including Stanley Ho, Li Ka-shing, Nicholas Tse, Charmaine Sheh and other celebrities, have also implemented Sheng Ji layouts. This art has always accompanied the changes of the times, being accepted and practiced by different people in different ways.

Life's fortune is not completely uncontrollable; the key is whether one is willing to plant seeds of good fortune for oneself in advance.
